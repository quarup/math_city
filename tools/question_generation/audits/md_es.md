# MathDataset-ElementarySchool (MD-ES) — ingestion audit

This document is the input to the dataset-ingestion sub-track decisions for
MD-ES. It examines every `(source, subcategory)` bucket, samples real items,
and assigns a verdict per bucket:

- **variety** — the underlying math is already covered by our algorithmic
  generators; ingesting adds *phrasing variety*.
- **gap-fill** — the math isn't covered by any current generator. High value
  to ingest (subject to license + format complexity).
- **redundant** — already on our priority dataset list (DeepMind / SVAMP /
  MathQA). Ingesting MD-ES would be a strict downgrade vs. ingesting the
  upstream directly (smaller / re-sampled / no seed control).
- **license_block** — the upstream license is missing, unclear, or NC.
  Not safe to bundle in v1.
- **out_of_scope** — outside CCSS K–8.

The raw sampled items live in [md_es_samples.md](md_es_samples.md), auto-
regenerated by [`audit_md_es.py`](../audit_md_es.py).

---

## Headline finding

**MD-ES is structurally a convenience re-bundling of 6 upstream datasets.
Every bucket falls into one of two categories:**

1. **Redundant with a dataset already on our §7.2 priority list** —
   DeepMind (14 buckets), SVAMP (1 bucket), MathQA (1 bucket). For each of
   these, ingesting MD-ES is strictly worse than ingesting the upstream
   directly: smaller samples, no seed control, an extra hop in the
   attribution chain.
2. **License-blocked** — the 3 buckets unique to MD-ES (Math-401, AddSub,
   MultiArith) all have missing or unclear licenses. Math-401 has no
   LICENSE file at all. AddSub and MultiArith both pull from MAWPS, which
   curriculum.md §7.4 already flagged as unclear; the MD-ES MIT license
   covers the repackaging scripts, not the underlying upstream data.

**Net recommendation:** **do not ingest from MD-ES at all.** Every slice
worth having is reachable via a cleaner path (direct DeepMind ingest,
SVAMP audit #5, MathQA audit #4); every slice not reachable that way is
license-blocked. The MD-ES audit collapses to "audit the upstreams
directly."

This is a useful negative result: it removes MD-ES from the §7.2
implementation queue and re-confirms that the only sources to chase from
this neighborhood are the GREEN upstreams already on our list.

## Why this audit is a "skip the dataset" verdict, not a "skip these buckets"

Every audit in our §7.2 series produces *some* ingestion-worthy slice — except this one. Worth being explicit about why MD-ES is the outlier:

1. **MD-ES is methodologically a re-bundling**, not a primary dataset. Its README says so: *"We followed a methodical approach… we identified datasets for each category."* The contribution is the categorisation work, not new items.

2. **The bundle's MIT license is on the repackaging scripts**, not the underlying upstream data. Each row inherits its upstream's license. For a free / app-store-distributed product (per [prd.md](../../prd.md)) that's a critical distinction: we need an unbroken chain of permissive licenses from creator → us. Re-bundling doesn't launder a missing upstream license.

3. **For the GREEN upstreams** (DeepMind, SVAMP, MathQA-Geometry), MD-ES is strictly worse than the upstream:
   - Smaller item counts (the `_1000` Arithmetic file = 1000 items across 14 buckets ≈ 71/bucket; the upstream DeepMind generator is unbounded).
   - No seed determinism (MD-ES is a one-time random sample of the upstreams).
   - One extra attribution step.

4. **For the unique-to-MD-ES upstreams** (Math-401, AddSub, MultiArith), the license trail dead-ends:
   - Math-401: no LICENSE file in the repo.
   - AddSub / MultiArith: routed through MAWPS, which is the §7.4-flagged unclear-license source. The MD-ES author's choice to bundle these doesn't make them safe for us to redistribute.

The "convenience" value MD-ES would provide — one-stop-shopping for elementary-math Q&A — isn't worth taking on the license-trail risk. **Audit verdict: skip MD-ES; revisit only if MAWPS publishes an explicit license.**

## Recommended next audit

Promote **SVAMP** (#5) to the front of the queue, since one of MD-ES's
1000-item slices was already SVAMP and the sample inspection confirmed
content quality is high. SVAMP's audit will go fast — single-source,
single license (MIT), grades 2–4. After SVAMP: **MathQA** (#4) for the
geometry slice. **GSM8K** (#2) remains the long-term highest-leverage
audit because it's the only GREEN source for rich grade 3–6 word
problems.

The MD-ES audit functionally cost us one branch; the trade is we now
know that MAWPS-derived word problems (a tempting K-2 content source)
are off-limits in v1.
